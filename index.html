Why we upgrade now

The web estate isn't ready for the moment it's about to meet.

Across both channels the picture is the same: strong intent arriving at pages that read as generic, inconsistent, and a step behind the regulation driving the demand. Four reasons it can't wait.

1

A compliance window that won't repeat

E-invoicing and continuous-compliance mandates are forcing every small business and their accountant to act — France from September 2026, Spain on VeriFactu & Crea y Crece, Germany's E-Rechnung receipt duty, Portugal's ATCUD / SAF-T regime. This is the largest demand event in years, and the web is where it lands.

2

An estate that doesn't feel local

Pan-European screenshots carry foreign names and stray typos, social proof is thin where it should be strongest, and Portugal has no Active page at all. It reads as generic at the exact moment buyers want local certainty about their own obligation.

3

Mixed messages, market to market

Promo end-dates drift between countries, and the compliance hook that should lead every page is front-and-centre in France, thin in Spain and receive-only in Germany. Inconsistency erodes the trust that converts a worried buyer into a trial.

4

One standard, and a way to hold it

Each channel carries a playbook for what a "good" country page looks like — local regulatory clock, named compliance mechanism, real proof, one clear offer. Every demo is built to it and every live site measured against it, so the bar holds as we scale across markets.
